projection of right angle


Theorem.  The projection (http://planetmath.org/ProjectionOfPoint) of a right angle in ℝ3 onto a plane is a right angle if and only if at least one of its sides is parallel to the plane.

Proof.  Consider the projection of an angle α with vertex (http://planetmath.org/Angle) P onto the plane π.  Let P′ be the projection of P onto π.  If neither of the sides of α is parallel to π, then the lines of the sides intersect the plane in two distinct points A and B.  In order to that the angle of view of the segment A⁢B seen from the point P would be a right angle, P must be on a sphere with diameter A⁢B centered at a point O.  In order to that the projection angle A⁢P′⁢B would be a right angle, the point P′ must be on a circle of the plane π having A⁢B as diameter.  But O⁢P′ is as the projection of the segment O⁢P shorter than O⁢P.  It follows that the angle A⁢P′⁢B is obtuse and hence cannot be right.
On the other hand, it’s not hard to see that the projection of a right angle is a right angle always when one or both of its sides are parallel to the projection plane.
