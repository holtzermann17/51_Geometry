proof of Pythagorean theorem


Let A⁢B⁢C be a right triangle with hypotenuse B⁢C. Draw the height A⁢T.

Using the right angles ∠⁢B⁢A⁢C and ∠⁢A⁢T⁢B and the fact that the sum of angles on any triangle is 180∘, it can be shown that

∠⁢B⁢A⁢T = ∠⁢A⁢C⁢T
∠⁢T⁢A⁢C = ∠⁢C⁢B⁢A

and therefore we have the following triangle similarities:

△⁢A⁢B⁢C∼△⁢T⁢B⁢A∼△⁢T⁢A⁢C.

From those similarities, we have A⁢BB⁢C=T⁢BB⁢A and thus A⁢B2=B⁢C⋅T⁢B. Also A⁢CB⁢C=T⁢CA⁢C and thus A⁢C2=B⁢C⋅T⁢C. We have then

A⁢B2+A⁢C2=B⁢C⁢(B⁢T+T⁢C)=B⁢C⋅B⁢C=B⁢C2

which concludes the proof.
