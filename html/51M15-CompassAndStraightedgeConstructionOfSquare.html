compass and straightedge construction of square


One can construct a square with sides of a given length s using compass and straightedge as follows:

  1. 1.

    Draw a line segment of length s. Label its endpoints P and Q.

  2. 2.

    Extend the line segment past Q.

  3. 3.

    Erect the perpendicular to P⁢Q→ at Q.

  4. 4.

    Using the line drawn in the previous step, mark off a line segment of length s such that one of its endpoints is Q. Label the other endpoint as R.

  5. 5.

    Draw an arc of the circle with center P and radius P⁢Q¯.

  6. 6.

    Draw an arc of the circle with center R and radius Q⁢R¯ to find the point S where it intersects the arc from the previous step such that S≠Q.

  7. 7.

    Draw the square P⁢Q⁢R⁢S.

This construction is justified because P⁢S=P⁢Q=Q⁢R=Q⁢S, yielding that P⁢Q⁢R⁢S is a rhombus. Since ∠⁢P⁢Q⁢R is a right angle, it follows that P⁢Q⁢R⁢S is a square.
