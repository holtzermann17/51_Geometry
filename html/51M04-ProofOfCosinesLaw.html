proof of cosines law


Let a, b, c be the sides of a triangle and α, β, γ its angles, respectively.  By the projection formula, one may write the equalities

{a=b⁢cos⁡γ+c⁢cos⁡βb=c⁢cos⁡α+a⁢cos⁡γc=a⁢cos⁡β+b⁢cos⁡α.

Multiplying the equalities by a, -b and -c, respectively, they read

{a2=a⁢b⁢cos⁡γ+c⁢a⁢cos⁡β-b2=-b⁢c⁢cos⁡α-a⁢b⁢cos⁡γ-c2=-c⁢a⁢cos⁡β-b⁢c⁢cos⁡α.

Addition of these yields the sum equation

a2-b2-c2=-2⁢b⁢c⁢cos⁡α,

i.e.

a2=b2+c2-2⁢b⁢c⁢cos⁡α,

which is the cosines law.
