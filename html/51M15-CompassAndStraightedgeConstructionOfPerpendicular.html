compass and straightedge construction of perpendicular


Let P be a point and ℓ be a line in the Euclidean plane. One can construct a line m perpendicular to ℓ and passing through P. The construction given here yields m in any circumstance: Whether P∈ℓ or P∉ℓ does not matter. On the other hand, the construction looks quite different in these two cases. Thus, the sequence of pictures on the left (in which ℓ is in red) is for the case that P∉ℓ, and the sequence of pictures on the right (in which ℓ is in green) is for the case that P∈ℓ.

  1. 1.

    With one point of the compass on P, draw an arc that intersects ℓ at two points. Label these as Q and R.

  2. 2.

    Construct the perpendicular bisector of Q⁢R¯. This line is m.

This construction is justified because Q and R are constructed so that P is equidistant from them and thus lies on the perpendicular bisector of Q⁢R¯.

In the case that P∉ℓ, this construction is referred to as dropping the perpendicular from P to ℓ. In the case that P∈ℓ, this construction is referred to as erecting the perpendicular to ℓ at P.
