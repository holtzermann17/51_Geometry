Ptolemy’s theorem


If A⁢B⁢C⁢D is a cyclic quadrilateral, then the product of the two diagonals is equal to the sum of the products of opposite sides.

A⁢C⋅B⁢D=A⁢B⋅C⁢D+A⁢D⋅B⁢C.

When the quadrilateral is not cyclic we have the following inequality

A⁢B⋅C⁢D+B⁢C⋅A⁢D>A⁢C⋅B⁢D

An interesting particular case is when both A⁢C and B⁢D are diameters, since we get another proof of Pythagoras’ theorem.
