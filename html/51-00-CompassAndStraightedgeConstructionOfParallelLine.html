compass and straightedge construction of parallel line


Task.  Construct the line parallel to a given line ℓ and passing through a given point P which is not on ℓ.

Solution.

  1. 1.

    Draw a circle c1 with center (http://planetmath.org/Center8) P and intersecting ℓ at two points, one of which is A.

  2. 2.

    Draw a second circle c2 with center A and the same radius r as c1. This circle also intersects ℓ at two points, one of which is B.

  3. 3.

    Draw a third circle c3 with center B and radius r.  Let C be the intersection point of c3 (drawn below in red) with c1 (drawn below in green) which lies on the same side of ℓ as P does.  The line P⁢C (drawn below in blue) is the required parallel to ℓ.

Note 1.  The construction is based on the fact that the quadrilateral P⁢A⁢B⁢C is a parallelogram. In fact, P⁢A⁢B⁢C is a rhombus. The reasoning is as follows:

  • •

    The green circle shows that P⁢C¯ and P⁢A¯ are congruent.

  • •

    The black circle shows that P⁢A¯ and A⁢B¯ are congruent.

  • •

    The red circle shows that A⁢B¯ and B⁢C¯ are congruent.

  • •

    Since  P⁢A⁢B⁢C  is a quadrilateral with all sides congruent, it is a rhombus (and therefore a parallelogram).

Note 2.  It is clear that the construction only needs the compass, not a straightedge: In determining the point C, the straightedge is totally superfluous, and the points P and C determine the desired line (which thus is not necessary to actually draw!). It may be proved that all constructions with compass and straightedge are possible using only the compass.
