quadrant


The x- and y-axes the x⁢y-plane in four right-angle domains which are called the quadrants of the plane.  They are numbered going round the origin in anticlockwise direction so that

{(x,y)⁢∣x>⁢0,y>0}

is the first quadrant,

{(x,y)∣x<0,y>0}

the second quadrant, and so on.

Naturally, one can speak of the quadrants of the complex plane, too.

The lines y=±x have as their slope angles ±45∘, thus halving the quadrant angles; they are called the quadrant bisectors.  Cf. angle bisector as locus.
