endpoint


An endpoint of a line segment L is a point that belongs to the boundary of L. Note that every line segment has two distinct endpoints. For example, if V is a vector space and a,b∈V with b≠0, then the endpoints of the line segment L={a+t⁢b:t∈[0,1]} are a and a+b.

Note that the endpoints of the open line segment L={a+t⁢b:t∈(0,1)} are also a and a+b.

Endpoints can be defined in a manner for other geometric . These include rays, arcs (http://planetmath.org/PathConnected), and intervals (http://planetmath.org/Interval).
