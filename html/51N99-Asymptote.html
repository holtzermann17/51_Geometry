asymptote


If a plane curve γ has a continuing infinitely far from the origin O, then γ may have an asymptote:  The direct line l is an asymptote of γ, if

limd⁢(P,O)→∞⁡d⁢(P,l)=0,

where d⁢(P,O) means the of the point P of the from the origin and d⁢(P,l) the of P from the line l.

Examples:  The hyperbola   x2a2-y2b2=1  has the asymptotes  y=±ba⁢x;  the curve   y=sin⁡xx  the asymptote  y=0.
