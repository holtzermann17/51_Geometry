Euler line


In any triangle, the orthocenter H, the centroid G and the circumcenter O are collinear, and O⁢G/G⁢H=1/2. The line passing by these points is known as the Euler line of the triangle.

This line also passes by the center of the nine-point circle (or Feuerbach circle) N, and N is the midpoint of O⁢H.
