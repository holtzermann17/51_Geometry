unit hyperbola


The unit hyperbola (cf. the unit circle) is the special case

x2-y2=1

of the hyperbola

x2a2-y2b2=1

where both the a and the b have equal to 1.  The unit hyperbola is rectangular, i.e. its asymptotes (y=±x) are at right angles to each other.

The unit hyperbola has the well-known parametric

x=±cosh⁡t,y=sinh⁡t,

and also a trigonometric

x=sec⁡t,y=tan⁡t.

The former yields the rational

x=u2+12⁢u,y=u2-12⁢u

when one substitutes  et=u, and the latter, via the substitution  tan⁡t2=u, the rational

x=1+u21-u2,y=2⁢u1-u2

(which does not give the left apex of the hyperbola).
