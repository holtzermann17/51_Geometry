Lobachevsky’s formula


Let A⁢B be a line. Let M,T be two points so that M not lies on A⁢B, T lies on A⁢B, and M⁢T perpendicular to A⁢B. Let M⁢D be any other line who meets A⁢T in D.In a hyperbolic geometry, as D moves off to infinity along A⁢T the line M⁢D meets the line M⁢S which is said to be parallel to A⁢T. The angle S⁢M⁢T^ is called the angle of parallelism for perpendicular distance d, and is given by

P⁢(d)=2⁢tan-1⁡(e-d),

which is called Lobachevsky’s formula.
