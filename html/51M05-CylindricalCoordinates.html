cylindrical coordinates


Cylindrical coordinates are a system of coordinates for ℝ3. Two of the coordinates correspond to the polar coordinates of ℝ2, and the third coordinate corresponds with the z axis. Thus, the coordinates are given by

(xyz)=(r⁢cos⁡θr⁢sin⁡θz),

where r is the distance from (0,0,0) to (x,y,0) and θ is the azimuthal angle defined for θ∈[0,2⁢π).

Just as with polar coordinates, one can convert from Cartesian coordinates to cylindrical coordinates for any point not lying on the z axis via

r⁢(x,y) = x2+y2,
θ⁢(x,y) = arctan⁡(x,y),

where arctan is defined here (http://planetmath.org/OperatornamearcTanWithTwoArguments).
