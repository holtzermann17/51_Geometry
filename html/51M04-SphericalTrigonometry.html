Cosine law.

In the following we deduce the cosine law for a spherical trihedron.

Let 𝐞𝟏,𝐞𝟐,𝐞𝟑 be the vertex unitary vectors as shown in the figure.

The cosine of the angle α formed by the plane defined by 𝐞𝟏,𝐞𝟐 and the plane defined by 𝐞𝟏,𝐞𝟑 is:

cos⁡α=(𝐞𝟏×𝐞𝟑)⋅(𝐞𝟏×𝐞𝟐)∥𝐞𝟏×𝐞𝟑∥⁢∥𝐞𝟏×𝐞𝟐∥=(𝐞𝟏×𝐞𝟑)⋅(𝐞𝟏×𝐞𝟐)sin⁡b⁢sin⁡c

Now, using the cyclic property of the triple vector product and Lagrange’s formula (http://planetmath.org/TripleCrossProduct), we can write:

cos⁡α=𝐞𝟏⋅(𝐞𝟑×(𝐞𝟏×𝐞𝟐))sin⁡b⁢sin⁡c=𝐞𝟏⋅((𝐞𝟑⋅𝐞𝟐)⁢𝐞𝟏-(𝐞𝟑⋅𝐞𝟏)⁢𝐞𝟐)sin⁡b⁢sin⁡c=cos⁡a-cos⁡b⁢cos⁡csin⁡b⁢sin⁡c

Hence:

cos⁡a=cos⁡b⁢cos⁡c+sin⁡b⁢sin⁡c⁢cos⁡α
