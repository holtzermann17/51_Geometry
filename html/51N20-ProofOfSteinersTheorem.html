proof of Steiner’s theorem


Using α,β,γ,δ to denote angles as in the diagram at left, the sines law yields

A⁢Bsin⁡(γ) = A⁢Csin⁡(β) (1)
N⁢Bsin⁡(α+δ) = N⁢Asin⁡(β) (2)
M⁢Csin⁡(α+δ) = M⁢Asin⁡(γ) (3)
M⁢Bsin⁡(α) = M⁢Asin⁡(β) (4)
N⁢Csin⁡(α) = N⁢Asin⁡(γ) (5)

Dividing (2) and (3), and (4) by (5):

M⁢AN⁢A⁢N⁢BM⁢C=sin⁡(γ)sin⁡(β)=N⁢AM⁢A⁢M⁢BN⁢C

and therefore

N⁢B⋅M⁢BM⁢C⋅N⁢C=sin2⁡(γ)sin2⁡(β)=A⁢B2A⁢C2

by (1).
