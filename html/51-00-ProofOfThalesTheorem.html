proof of Thales’ theorem


Let M be the center of the circle through A, B and C.

Then A⁢M=B⁢M=C⁢M and thus the triangles A⁢M⁢C and B⁢M⁢C are isosceles. If ∠BMC=:α then ∠⁢M⁢C⁢B=90∘-α2 and ∠⁢C⁢M⁢A=180∘-α. Therefore ∠⁢A⁢C⁢M=α2 and

∠⁢A⁢C⁢B=∠⁢M⁢C⁢B+∠⁢A⁢C⁢M=90∘.

QED.
