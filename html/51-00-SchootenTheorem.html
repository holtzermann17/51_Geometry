Schooten theorem


Theorem: Let A⁢B⁢C be a equilateral triangle. If M is a point on the circumscribed circle then the equality

A⁢M=B⁢M+C⁢M

holds.

Proof: Let B′∈(M⁢A) so that M⁢B′=B′⁢B. Because B⁢M⁢A^=B⁢C⁢A^=60∘, the triangle M⁢B⁢B′ is equilateral, so B⁢B′=M⁢B=M⁢B′. Because A⁢B=B⁢C,B⁢B′=B⁢M and A⁢B⁢B′^≡M⁢B⁢C^ we have that the triangles A⁢B⁢B′ and C⁢B⁢M are equivalent. Since M⁢C=A⁢B′ we have that A⁢M=A⁢B′+B′⁢M=M⁢C+M⁢B. □
