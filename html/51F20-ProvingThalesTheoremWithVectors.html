proving Thales’ theorem with vectors


Let the radius of the circle be r and A⁢B a diameter of the circle. We make the dot product calculation

P⁢A→⋅P⁢B→=(P⁢O→+O⁢A→)⋅(P⁢O→+O⁢B→)=(P⁢O→+O⁢A→)⋅(P⁢O→-O⁢A→)=P⁢O→⋅P⁢O→-O⁢A→⋅O⁢A→=r2-r2=0.

The result shows that  P⁢A→⟂P⁢B→, i.e. the circumferential angle A⁢P⁢B of the half-circle is a right angle.
