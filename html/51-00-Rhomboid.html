rhomboid


A rhomboid is a parallelogram that is neither a rhombus nor a rectangle. The word rhomboid comes from the Greek word ρ⁢o⁢μ⁢β⁢o⁢ε⁢ι⁢δ⁢η´⁢ς, which is transliterated as ‘rhomboeidis’. In The Elements, Euclid has this definition:

“Of quadrilateral figures….a rhomboid (is) that which has its opposite sides and angles equal to each other but is neither equilateral (http://planetmath.org/Equilateral) nor right-angled (http://planetmath.org/RightAngle).”
