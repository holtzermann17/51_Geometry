centroid


The centroid of a triangle (also called center of gravity of the triangle) is the point where the three medians intersect each other.

In the figure, A⁢A′,B⁢B′ and C⁢C′ are medians and G is the centroid of A⁢B⁢C. The centroid G has the property that divides the medians in the ratio 2:1, that is

A⁢G=2⁢G⁢A′ B⁢G=2⁢G⁢B′ C⁢G=2⁢G⁢C′.
