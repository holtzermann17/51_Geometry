defect


Consider a triangle △⁢A⁢B⁢C in either hyperbolic or spherical geometry (http://planetmath.org/NonEuclideanGeometry) in which its angle sum in radians is Σ.

In hyperbolic geometry, the defect of △⁢A⁢B⁢C is δ⁢(△⁢A⁢B⁢C)=π-Σ.

In spherical geometry, the defect of △⁢A⁢B⁢C is δ⁢(△⁢A⁢B⁢C)=Σ-π.

Note that, in both hyperbolic and spherical geometry, the area of a is equal to its defect.
