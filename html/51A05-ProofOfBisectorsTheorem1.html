proof of bisectors theorem


Consider sines law in triangles △⁢A⁢P⁢B and △⁢A⁢P⁢C.

On △⁢A⁢P⁢B we have

B⁢Psin⁡B⁢A⁢P=A⁢Bsin⁡A⁢P⁢B

and on △⁢A⁢P⁢C we have

P⁢Csin⁡P⁢A⁢C=A⁢Csin⁡C⁢P⁢A.

Combining the two relation gives

B⁢PP⁢C=A⁢B⁢sin⁡B⁢A⁢P/sin⁡A⁢P⁢BA⁢C⁢sin⁡P⁢A⁢C/sin⁡C⁢P⁢A.

However, ∠⁢A⁢P⁢B+∠⁢C⁢P⁢A=180∘, and so sin⁡A⁢P⁢B=sin⁡C⁢P⁢A. Cancelling gives

B⁢PP⁢C=A⁢B⁢sin⁡B⁢A⁢PA⁢C⁢sin⁡P⁢A⁢C,

which is the generalization of the theorem. When A⁢P is a bisector, ∠⁢B⁢A⁢P=∠⁢P⁢A⁢C and we can cancel further to obtain the bisector theorem.
