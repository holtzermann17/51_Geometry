incenter


The incenter of a geometrical shape is the center of the incircle (if it has any). The radius of the incircle is sometimes called the inradius.

On a triangle the incenter always exists and it is the intersection point of the three internal angle bisectors. So in the next picture, A⁢X,B⁢Y,C⁢Z are angle bisectors, and A⁢B,B⁢C,C⁢A are tangent to the circle.
