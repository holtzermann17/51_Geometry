explementary


The explementary arc of an arc a of a circle is the arc forming together with a the full circle.

Two angles are called explementary angles of each other, if their sum is the full angle 2⁢π, i.e. 360∘. In the below picture, the  α=60∘  of an equilateral triangle and its explementary angle  β=300∘  (which is an of the triangle) are seen.
